Dungeon Mode

Dungeon Mode is Anime Card Clash's PvE floor-climb: everything in your normal inventory gets locked away, and you start from nothing, building a fresh deck out of Dungeon Instant Rolls as you fight your way down. Whatever you find down there stays down there — but the coins, the merchant, and the luck you build up along the way make every run worth the trip.

Core Mechanics

What makes a dungeon run different

Fresh Start

Nothing carries in

Your main inventory is disabled for the run. You build a brand-new deck using Dungeon Instant Rolls, starting with a batch of 100,000 the moment you step in.

Nothing carries out

Everything stays down there

Cards, levels, and traits you pull inside the dungeon are dungeon-only. Your original card levels and traits never apply here, and nothing you earn transfers back to your main collection.

Independent Luck

A separate luck stat

Dungeon luck is tracked on its own — it only moves with dungeon-level upgrades and active modifiers, not anything from your main profile.

Floor Progression

How the climb scales

Floors 11–20Reward items ×2
Every 10 floorsMerge Machine spawns

Reward tiers keep stacking the deeper you go, so the value of each individual floor climbs even as the fights get harder.

Enemy scaling

Enemies per floor1–4 per player, minimum 1
Max enemy team sizeUp to 6 cards
Deeper floorsHigher Luck stat per floor reached

Everyone in your dungeon party has to reach the next floor's entrance before the next floor actually begins — no one can pull the group ahead alone.

Party & Survival

Lives, co-op, and AFK runs

Co-op

1–4 players

Dungeons support solo play or a party of up to four, and the whole group shares one pool of lives for the run.

Lives

Shared, and limited

A lost battle costs the party one life. When the shared pool runs out, the dungeon ends and you have to start a fresh one to keep climbing.

AFK Mode

Toggle it on or off

AFK dungeon settings can auto-battle the next enemy, auto-teleport to the next floor, and auto-use roll packs, so a run can progress with minimal input if you set it up first.

Modifier votes

TriggerEvery 5 floors

DurationApplies for the rest of the run

Vote as a group — the chosen modifier locks in for everyone until the run ends, win or lose.

The Dungeon Merchant

Spending what you earn

The Dungeon Merchant only accepts Dungeon Coins, which you build up from mini-boss and boss clears as you descend. Coins can be spent on Dungeon Craftable Cards — cards that only become rollable in your dungeon deck once you've crafted them — along with the exclusive Giant cards that unlock as you pass certain floor thresholds. Because most of those Giant cards have a stock of one, it's worth deciding in advance which unlock you actually want before you spend down your coin stash.

Dungeon Variants

Titan Dungeon & Bizarre Dungeon

Titan Dungeon

Difficulty challenges & modifiers

A harder variant layered on top of the base dungeon loop, with its own difficulty challenges and a dedicated modifier list. Treat it as the place to push a deck you've already proven can clear a standard run comfortably.

Bizarre Dungeon

Stand mechanics

Introduces its own special "Stand" mechanic on top of the usual difficulty challenges and modifier lists, changing how cards interact compared to a normal or Titan run.

Tutorial

Running your first dungeon

Step 01

Enter with a clear goal

Decide up front whether you're chasing floor depth, Dungeon Coins, or a specific Giant card unlock. Your starting 100,000 Dungeon Instant Rolls go further if you're not splitting focus between three goals at once.

Step 02

Build your fresh deck fast

Since none of your main-game cards apply, spend your opening rolls on the strongest deck you can assemble immediately rather than saving rolls for later — every floor you clear pays out more rolls anyway, so the deck compounds as you go.

Step 03

Coordinate lives with your party

Because the whole party shares one pool of lives, agree on when to play safe versus when to push a risky floor. One bad pull for one player still costs everyone a life.

Step 04

Vote modifiers with the run in mind

When the every-5-floor modifier vote comes up, pick the buff that supports the goal you set in Step 01 — a coin-farming run and a depth-farming run usually want different modifiers.

Step 05

Spend at the Merchant before you leave

Dungeon Coins don't do anything sitting unspent between runs. Check the Merchant for any newly unlocked Giant cards before you back out, since some of those slots only carry a single unit in stock.
