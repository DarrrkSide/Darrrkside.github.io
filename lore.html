The Reedwarden's Almanac

A chapter-by-chapter account of how the kingdom of Vessport went under, who bonded with what came up out of the water, and why the mire keeps asking questions of everyone who wades in.

Chapter 01

The Last Dry Year

Vessport was a trade kingdom built on a floodplain, and everyone who lived there knew the water would eventually take it back — they simply expected centuries, not a season. The chronicles call the final year "dry" only because the rain hadn't started yet. It's the last entry written before the ink itself began to run.

Court records from that year describe unexplained lantern-outages, wells that reversed their flow, and a growing number of townsfolk who stopped sleeping altogether, claiming something under the floorboards was "counting." Historians now believe this was the mere testing its reach before the flood proper.

"We did not drown in a night. We drowned in the time it takes to decide not to leave." — Court Archivist, final entry
Lore Note The in-game "Founder's Bell" you can ring in the Vessport ruins plays the exact toll pattern described in this chapter's court records — one of the few sound cues confirmed to be historically accurate within the game's own fiction.
Chapter 02

The First Bonding

When the floodwaters finally came, they didn't simply rise — they brought something with them. Survivors reported spirits rising out of the new lake, formless until a drowning person reached for one. Those who reached and held on became the first Cards. Those who let go were never seen again.

The bonding wasn't gentle. Early Cards describe it less as a partnership and more as a negotiated survival — the spirit kept you breathing, and in exchange you carried it, fed it, and eventually let it use your hands. That uneasy trade is still the basis of every Card bond in the game today.

Lore Note This is why every Card's bio in-game lists a "bonded name" and a separate "given name" — the given name is who they were, the bonded name is what the mere calls them now.
Chapter 03

The Reed Cards

The first organized faction, formed by survivors who bonded with slow, patient spirits — the kind that held riverbanks together rather than tearing them apart. Reed Cards don't hit hard, but nothing outlasts them, and in a world that rewards patience over speed, that made them the closest thing Vessport has to a standing army.

Their creed is short enough to fit on the practice-yard banners still standing in the flooded district: hold the line, let the water do the rest. It's also good gameplay advice — most beginner-friendly team builds lean on a Reed Card front line for exactly this reason.

Chapter 04

The Sunken Choir

Where the Reed Cards sought to endure the mere, the Sunken Choir sought to please it. They sing to the deepest parts of the flood in a language no living scholar has fully translated, and in return, the mere occasionally answers with something powerful, unstable, and hungry. Nearly all Mythic-tier Cards trace their bonding back to a Choir ritual.

"Ask nicely enough, and the water gives you a monster. Ask too many times, and it keeps the favor." — Choir defector's confession, undated
Lore Note The game's pity system is a direct mechanical nod to this — the "favor" the mere keeps is the pull counter resetting only once you're guaranteed a result, win or lose.
Chapter 05

Ashlight and its Debts

The Ashlight Covenant didn't bond with the mere at all — they bonded with light itself, or at least a stubborn refusal to let it go out. Their lanterns are the only reliable illumination in the flooded districts, and they charge for it, in favors, in salvage, and occasionally in years.

Neither friend nor enemy to the other factions, Ashlight traders show up in nearly every questline as the toll-keeper between zones. Players tend to either love or resent them depending entirely on how much salvage they've had to hand over.

Chapter 06

The Quiet Tide

Not everyone who drowned in Vessport became a Card or a monster. Some simply stayed, unbonded, unchanged, and mostly silent — the Quiet Tide. They remember the kingdom exactly as it was, which makes them the most reliable source of pre-flood history and the most unsettling faction to actually talk to.

Lore Note Nearly every "hidden lore" collectible in the game is voiced, in text, by an unnamed member of the Quiet Tide. If a note reads as oddly specific about a building that no longer exists, that's why.
Chapter 07

What the Mere Wants

No faction, including the Choir, claims to actually know what the mere is asking for when it "counts," tests, and occasionally rewards the people living on top of it. The closest thing to a consensus, across four factions who agree on almost nothing else, is that it isn't finished asking.

That's the thread every current story update pulls on, and it's worth keeping an eye on future chapters — whatever the mere wants, the game seems to be building toward finally answering it.
